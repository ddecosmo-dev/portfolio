U.S. Army Senior Capstone Project

Timeline: August 2024 - May 2025
Team Size: 6 Members (Led)
Delivery: 1 Month Early

Led a multidisciplinary team of six engineers in designing a reusable modular target system for U.S. Army military exercises. This capstone project demonstrates leadership, systems engineering, and the ability to deliver complex hardware projects on an accelerated timeline.

Project Highlights

  • Led 6-person engineering team through full project lifecycle
  • Gathered comprehensive military requirements from stakeholders
  • Designed modular target geometries with reusability as core design principle
  • Delivered complete system with assembly and maintenance documentation 1 month ahead of schedule

Project Overview

This capstone project addresses a real need within the U.S. Army: designing a modular, reusable target system that can be deployed in various military exercise scenarios. The system needed to be versatile enough to accommodate different types of exercises while maintaining consistency, durability, and ease of assembly/disassembly by field personnel.

Key Responsibilities & Achievements

Requirements Gathering & Stakeholder Management
Conducted extensive interviews and workshops with Army personnel to understand the diverse needs of military exercises. Translated stakeholder feedback into precise engineering requirements covering durability, portability, modularity, cost constraints, and maintenance cycles.
Team Leadership
Directed a 6-person interdisciplinary team through the complete project lifecycle including conception, design, prototyping, testing, and documentation. Coordinated team efforts, managed timelines, resolved design conflicts, and ensured alignment with project objectives and Army requirements.
Systems Design
Developed modular target geometries that could be reconfigured for different exercise types while sharing common components and assembly principles. The design prioritized field-maintainability, allowing soldiers to assemble, repair, and reconfigure targets with minimal training and tools.
Technical Documentation
Created comprehensive documentation package including:
  • Assembly procedures with visual guides for field personnel
  • Maintenance schedules and procedures
  • Component specifications and material requirements
  • Troubleshooting guides for common field issues
  • Design rationale documentation for future modifications
Accelerated Delivery
Through effective project management, team coordination, and strategic design decisions, the team delivered the complete system one month ahead of the scheduled timeline without compromising quality or requirements fulfillment.

Design Principles

  • Modularity: Components designed for easy reconfiguration and reuse across different scenarios
  • Durability: Materials and construction methods specified for harsh field environments
  • Maintainability: Design emphasizes field repair capability with minimal tools required
  • Cost Efficiency: Economies of scale through reusable components and simplified manufacturing
  • User-Centered Design: Continuous feedback from Army personnel integrated throughout development

Project Competencies

Systems Engineering Requirements Analysis CAD Design Project Management Team Leadership Mechanical Design Technical Documentation Stakeholder Management

Project Outcomes

  • Delivery Status: Completed 1 month ahead of schedule
  • Documentation: Comprehensive assembly, maintenance, and design documentation provided
  • Team Size Managed: 6 engineers with diverse specializations
  • Stakeholder Satisfaction: All Army requirements successfully met and validated
  • System Performance: Modular design proven effective across multiple exercise scenarios

Leadership Lessons

This project demonstrated the importance of clear communication, early stakeholder engagement, and thoughtful systems design. By focusing on modularity and user needs, the team created a solution that not only met current requirements but positioned the Army for future flexibility. The successful acceleration of the timeline was achieved through effective planning and team coordination rather than cutting scope or quality.
