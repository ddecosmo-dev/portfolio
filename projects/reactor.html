Zigzag Flow Reactor

Timeline: January 2022 - May 2025
Type: Honors Thesis
Academic: ASU Barrett Honors College

ASU Barrett Honors Thesis project: Redesigned mechanical and electrical systems for a laboratory reactor used to characterize CAM28 energy storage medium. Research identified faster reduction reactions than previously documented, advancing the understanding of this thermochemical storage systems.

Back to Projects

Project Highlights

  • Redesigned reactor mechanical and electrical systems increasing test lifetime by 2 hours
  • Identified faster reduction reactions than previously documented in literature
  • Determined that CAM28 can undergo 2x as many reduction cycles as previously tested

Project Overview

For my honors thesis at ASU, I improved the design of a thermochemical energy storage reactor and characterized the performance of CAM28 (a composite of calcium, aluminum, and magnesium oxides) storage material. The project involved a comprehensive system redesign to optimize experimental conditions and enable more detailed characterization of the material's reduction kinetics.

Key Contributions

Mechanical System Redesign
Evaluated and improved the reactor's mechanical architecture to better control reaction conditions, ensure thermal uniformity, and facilitate accurate measurement of reaction kinetics. Design modifications focused on maintaining consistent temperature profiles and minimizing heat losses to the environment.
Electrical System Optimization
Redesigned electrical control and monitoring systems to enable precise temperature control and real-time data acquisition. Improvements provided better feedback for understanding reaction progression and enabled more sophisticated experimental protocols.
Research Findings
Experimental results demonstrated faster reduction reaction rates in CAM28 than previously reported in literature. Used to inform future design iterations of the reactor.
Experimental Methodology
Developed robust experimental procedures for characterizing the material across various operating conditions. Created detailed protocols for temperature ramping, data collection, and analysis that enable reproducible results and comparison with other researchers' findings.
