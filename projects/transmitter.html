Project Overview

This internship project involved designing custom components for an Air Force threat emitter system at the Tobyhanna Army Depot. The work required understanding complex military specifications, managing technical constraints, and coordinating with multiple stakeholders to deliver precision components suitable for defense applications.

Key Achievements

Component Design
Designed four specialized transmitter components each meeting distinct functional and environmental requirements. Each component required careful consideration of electrical performance, mechanical integrity, manufacturability, and alignment with system-level threat emitter specifications.
Requirements Analysis
Gathered and analyzed detailed specifications from Air Force stakeholders and system engineers. Translated high-level operational requirements into precise engineering constraints covering dimensions, materials, electrical characteristics, and environmental performance.
CAD & Detailed Design
Produced comprehensive CAD models and detailed engineering drawings with full annotations and specifications. Designs incorporated manufacturing best practices to ensure producibility while maintaining tight tolerances required for defense applications.
Project Execution
Successfully managed the design-to-delivery timeline, coordinating with manufacturing, quality assurance, and procurement teams. Through efficient scheduling and proactive problem-solving, delivered all four components 2 weeks ahead of the agreed schedule.
Cost Management
Achieved $1,000 cost savings on the component package through strategic design optimization and smart procurement decisions. Savings were realized without compromising performance, reliability, or quality—demonstrating effective cost engineering.

Design Considerations

  • Military Specifications: All designs met or exceeded applicable military standards (MIL-SPEC) for materials, processes, and performance
  • Environmental Performance: Components designed for wide temperature ranges and harsh operational environments
  • Manufacturing: Designs optimized for commercial manufacturing processes while maintaining precision requirements
  • Integration: Custom components designed to integrate seamlessly with existing threat emitter system architecture
  • Reliability: Conservative design margins and material selection ensured high reliability
